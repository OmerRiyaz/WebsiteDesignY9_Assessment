History
Switzerland originates from the Old Swiss Confederacy established in the Late Middle Ages, following a series of military successes against Austria and Burgundy; the Federal Charter of 1291 is considered the country’s founding document1. Switzerland became a federal republic in 1848 and has maintained its neutrality in world wars2. Switzerland has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, influenced by its four official languages (German, French, Italian and Romansh) and its neighbours. Switzerland has also been a pioneer in democracy, human rights, science, education and innovation.
Location
Switzerland is a landlocked country in central Europe, bordered by France, Germany, Italy, Austria and Liechtenstein2. It has an area of about 41,285 square kilometers (15,940 square miles)2. Switzerland is divided into 26 cantons, which are further subdivided into municipalities. Switzerland lies mostly within the Alps, a mountain range that stretches across Europe. The highest point in Switzerland is the Dufourspitze (4,634 meters or 15,203 feet) in the Monte Rosa massif2
Population
The estimated population of Switzerland in 2023 is 8.86 million2, making it the 99th most populous country in the world. The population density is about 214 people per square kilometer (554 per square mile)2. The largest city is Zurich, with about 1.5 million people. Other major cities include Geneva, Basel, Lausanne and Bern. The population of Switzerland is composed of various ethnic groups, mainly Swiss (about 74%), German (about 15%), Italian (about 4%), French (about 3%) and others2. The majority of the population is Christian (about 65%), followed by non-religious (about 25%), Muslim (about 5%) and others2.

Capital City
The de facto capital of Switzerland is Bern, which is also the seat of the federal government and parliament. Bern has a population of about 133,000 (as of 2022). Bern is located on the Aare river, in the Swiss Plateau region. Bern is known for its medieval old town, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Some of the landmarks in Bern include the Zytglogge (a clock tower), the Bundeshaus (the federal palace), the Berner Münster (the cathedral) and the Bärengraben (the bear pit).
Seasons
Switzerland has four distinct seasons: spring (March to May), summer (June to August), autumn (September to November) and winter (December to February)2. The climate varies depending on the altitude and the region, ranging from temperate to alpine. Switzerland is known for its natural beauty and its ski resorts. Some of the popular tourist attractions in Switzerland include the Matterhorn (a famous mountain peak), the Jungfrau region (a scenic area with glaciers and lakes), the Rhine Falls (the largest waterfall in Europe), the Chillon Castle (a medieval fortress on Lake Geneva) and the Swiss National Park (a protected area with wildlife and hiking trails)2.
