Lederhosen
In Switzerland and especially in the Alpine regions, Lederhosen are traditional leather trousers worn by men. It's usually composed of mild leather, and it reaches below the knee. Lederhosen are often accompanied by a traditional shirt, suspenders, and sometimes a vest. This type of dress is usually worn during festivals, folk dances and cultural events.
Grisons Tracht
In eastern Switzerland, the Grisons Tracht are a classic dress worn by natives of the region. In addition to the women's long, pleated skirt, it has a bright blue waistcoat, an overcoat with puffed sleeves and an apron. The men's dress usually consists of black trousers, white shirt, vest and hat with a feather.
Dirndl
A Dirndl is a traditional women's clothing used in certain parts of Switzerland, and particularly in Germany speaking areas. It's usually made up of a bodice, a blouse, a full skirt, and an apron. With each individual region showing its own style, the colour and design of the dirndl can vary. Often it is accompanied by a puffed blouse with sleeves, decorative aprons or other accessories such as lace bodices, hat tops and traditional footwear.
Music & Dance
Its varied culture is reflected by the rich classical music and dance tradition of Switzerland. The Swiss Alps are a close link to yondeling, an extraordinary form of communication and music. In the mountains and in festivals, Alphorn's long wooden horn sings. Folk music and dances, such as the Landler, are accompanied by a lively Swiss accordion named Schwyzerergeli. Landler is a German folk dance, performed in pairs and with rhythmic movements. Switzerland's folk music is characterised by fiddles, accordions, and dulcimers, which vary from region to region. These forms of music and dance are a major cultural feature, connecting with the past as well as promoting celebration and unity.
